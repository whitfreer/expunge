Based on your answers, you were arrested as a juvenile.

However, you cannot apply to erase your juvenile record until at least 2 years (or 4 years for a serious juvenile offense) have passed from the date the court, or any other agency or institution the court sent you to, discharged you.

You may not have to wait 2 years (or 4 years for a serious juvenile offense) to petition for erasure of your juvenile record if, for example, you have had trouble getting a job, into college, housing, or the military because of your record.
